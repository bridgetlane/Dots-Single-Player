Dots (Single Player Version)

  • POLISHED CHANGES: This version of Dots is single player. The player plays against the computer. Improvements/polish include the AI computer player, restart/info buttons, score calculation, and ability to choose your colors.

  • High Concept: This version of Dots is single player. Connect the dots while keeping your opponents from doing so.

  • Player Experience Goals: The player experience should be fun and interactive. This time, I made the game single player, adding another way to play.

  • Genre: Casual games.

  • Game play: Pick your colors. The object of the game is to have the most completed rows/cols of your color at the end of the game.
    Select or deselect any circle on the board for your turn. You CANNOT pass your turn.
    If all 4 circles in a row or column are selected, the whole row/column will become locked (selected permanently).
    The COLOR of the last circle selected in a row/column will decide who that row/column goes to. When the last circle is selected, the entire row/col will become the color of that circle. This row/col is now UNMOVEABLE.
    At the end of the game, the player with the most completed rows/cols of their color wins.

    NOTE: Order matters. If you win a column, and a row is won, your column will be BROKEN by the row!
    System dynamics: The circles are unchangeable upon full row/column selection.
    Core mechanic: You can select and deselect circles.

  • Prototype goal: This prototype explores strategy mixed with some unpredictability. This prototype also explores making a digital game that is multi-player focused, minimizing technology and maximizing player-to-player interaction.

  • Features: My game is unique because a row or column you win can easily be lost.

  • Setting/Premise: None.

  • Story: None.

  • Target Audience: Casual gamers.

  • Play time: 3 minutes.

  • Strategies: Keep an eye on your main colors and which of them are selected.

  • Prototype Assets: Assets made by me.

  • Prototype Closest Other Game: I did not model this game or take the game framework from any existing game or Phaser example.
